Attribution Configuration Issues

The Goals summary shows two parallel purchase tracking systems running simultaneously. The primary "Memorialize Art (web) purchase" event uses Data-Driven Attribution and is recording conversions correctly. A secondary "Google Shopping App Purchase" event on Last Click attribution is also recording purchase conversions and contributing to bidding decisions. Both are set as Account Default Goals.

Running two purchase events simultaneously creates double-counting in reporting and sends conflicting attribution signals to Smart Bidding. The algorithm receives two different credit assignments for the same transaction and cannot accurately determine which ad interactions drove which outcomes.

All seven Google Shopping App events are eligible to switch to Data-Driven Attribution, but none have been migrated. The Switch to DDA notification has been visible in the account throughout Q4. With Last Click and Data-Driven events running in parallel, ROAS measurement is unreliable and the bidding model is receiving inconsistent signals across campaigns.

What This Means for PMax and Smart Bidding

Performance Max, Target ROAS, and Target CPA all rely on micro-conversion signals to understand the shape of the purchase path. Add to Cart identifies high-intent users. Begin Checkout weights those users more heavily for bid adjustment. Without either event, the bidding model treats every click with equal purchase probability regardless of actual user intent signals.

The Attribution Overview confirms this directly: 0% of conversion journeys in Q4 showed two or more ad interactions before converting. This is not because customers always converted on the first interaction. It reflects that the tracking infrastructure could not record multi-step paths correctly. Every campaign's Smart Bidding strategy made real-time bid decisions without this data for the entire quarter.

The practical consequence for ROAS: without mid-funnel signal, high-intent cart users and one-page bounces were bid on at similar rates. This over-spends on low-intent traffic and under-bids on the users who are closest to purchase. Both directions reduce ROAS, and the effect amplifies as budgets increase, which is consistent with performance patterns seen through November and December.

PMax Signal Fragmentation

Running five PMax campaigns simultaneously fragments the conversion signal each campaign receives. Google recommends that each PMax campaign accumulates at least 50 conversions per month to exit the learning phase and optimise reliably. Splitting the budget and signals across five campaigns means none of them reach that threshold at the individual campaign level.

The five PMax campaigns received between $1,731 and $29,622 in Q4 spend. At the lower budget levels, campaigns with 24 to 31 conversions over the quarter were permanently in a degraded learning state, making bid decisions with statistically insufficient data.

The one Excellent-rated PMax campaign (No Feed US at 2.96x) consistently outperformed the four Poor-rated ones. Asset quality is a direct lever on where PMax places inventory. Poor-rated asset groups get less competitive inventory. Rebuilding all asset groups to Excellent strength is a prerequisite for PMax to perform at its ceiling.

The Merchant Center Diagnostics screenshot shows all 12,238 products marked "Not eligible." The two flagged issues are "No campaigns advertising this product" affecting the full catalogue, and "Invalid price" on one product. The "No campaigns" error is a circular condition that occurs when all campaigns are paused. When campaign activity resumes, this status clears, but the invalid price issue must be resolved separately to avoid triggering broader feed quality reviews.

During the live Q4 period, the Shopping and feed-supplemented PMax campaigns were likely operating with reduced product eligibility. Standard Shopping returned 2.49x ROAS on $7,563 in Q4. A product feed with eligibility gaps prevents the Shopping algorithm from surfacing the most relevant products for each search query and forces it to work with an incomplete product subset, which limits conversion rates and increases CPA.

Impact on PMax Performance

Performance Max with a healthy product feed can serve Shopping ads across Google Search, Shopping, Display, Discover, Gmail, and YouTube simultaneously. PMax without a healthy feed is restricted to asset-only serving, which depends entirely on uploaded creative assets to find inventory. Two of the five Q4 PMax campaigns were explicitly feed-supplemented. If their feed was partially ineligible during live periods, their reach and performance would have been correspondingly constrained.

PMax Non-Brand Feed Only returned the highest PMax ROAS in the account at 3.24x. This campaign, designed specifically to serve feed inventory, outperformed the three other active feed campaigns despite receiving only $5,488 in Q4 spend. With a fully eligible feed and adequate budget, this campaign structure has room to become the account's primary acquisition vehicle.

Resolution Path

Resolving the feed requires: identifying and correcting the one invalid price product, reactivating campaigns to clear the "No campaigns" flag, conducting a full data quality audit across product titles, prices, availability, and images, and verifying that GTIN and brand attributes meet Google's requirements for custom art products.

Account Management Review

December Change History: A Structural Pattern

The change history covers July through December 2025. The December activity reveals a pattern of reactive bid and budget adjustments that is counterproductive for Smart Bidding stability. All changes in the account were made by a single user identifier associated with the previous management. The following documents the December sequence precisely as it appears in the change history data.

December 28, 2025 at 5:25 PM to 5:27 PM

Six changes made in two minutes

A single session produced: PMax No Feed US paused. Three concurrent budget reductions (Competitor Search $1,200 to $1,000 per day, Standard Search $1,200 to $1,000 per day, PMax All Products $2,063 to $2,000). Three concurrent ROAS target reductions (Standard Search 950% to 800%, Standard Shopping 850% to 800%, Non-Brand Feed PMax 900% to 800%). When a campaign is underperforming against a ROAS target, cutting the target further allows Smart Bidding to relax its efficiency threshold, which produces more volume but at lower returns. This trade-off is sometimes appropriate during scale-up phases but is typically not the right response to underperformance at established spend levels.

December 31, 2025 at 12:34 AM

Final changes before account transition

The last three account changes were: PMax All Products ROAS target raised from 600% to 800%, Competitor Search CPA target raised from $25 to $40, DSA CPA target raised from $25 to $40. Raising ROAS and CPA targets significantly on the final day of a management period means campaigns will enter a new learning cycle with highly restrictive bidding parameters on relaunch. A campaign with a tROAS target set to 800% needs considerably more conversion data before it can serve competitively, and it will restrict impression share aggressively until that data exists.

December 26 to 28, 2025

Campaign status cycling within 48 hours

PMax No Feed US was reactivated on December 26 and then paused again on December 28. PMax Non-Brand Feed Only was reactivated on December 26 and then had its ROAS target cut on December 28. Each time a campaign is paused and reactivated, the Smart Bidding algorithm resets its learning. A campaign that cycles through pause and reactivation within 48 hours at high spend levels never accumulates stable signal. The recommended practice is to avoid pausing active Smart Bidding campaigns unless there is a specific strategic reason, and to allow 2 to 4 weeks of stable running before evaluating performance.

Q4 2025 Overall Structure

Fifteen simultaneous campaigns targeting the same market

Five PMax, four Search, four Shopping, one DSA, and one Display campaign ran concurrently throughout Q4, all targeting the US. The naming convention applied to every campaign uses the "POA |" prefix, which refers to the management organisation rather than the client's business objectives. Beyond naming, running fifteen campaigns in a single market without a consolidation strategy means each one receives a fraction of the conversion signal it would need to optimise effectively at its individual budget level.

Ideal Customer Profile

Two Distinct Buyers. One Set of Ads. The Disconnect.

The Q4 data, combined with search term intent analysis, reveals two distinct buyer psychologies purchasing the same product for fundamentally different reasons. The current campaign structure does not segment creative, messaging, or landing pages between them. Building toward that separation is one of the highest-leverage improvements available without any budget increase.

Buyer Type 1: Grief-Led Purchase

Who they are: Female, 25 to 44. Has experienced a recent loss, typically within 6 to 24 months. Parent, partner, sibling, or pet. The purchase is for themselves or as a tribute gift to someone who shared the loss. Decision timeline is extended but intent is very high when it crystallises.

Search behaviour: "memorial paintings of loved ones," "portrait of deceased loved one," "painting of person who passed away," "add deceased loved one to family photo." High specificity, emotional language, clear outcome orientation. They are comparing suppliers, not product categories.

Primary concern: Trust. The fear of receiving a disappointing product at a significant emotional moment is the primary conversion barrier. Proof elements that matter most: process transparency, artist credentials, real customer reviews from people in similar situations, and explicit reassurance around the final result.

Conversion catalyst: Evidence that others in the same situation chose Memorialize Art and were satisfied. Reviews with emotional resonance, not just star ratings.

Buyer Type 2: Occasion-Led Gift

Who they are: Female or male, 25 to 44, purchasing a gift for someone who has experienced a loss. Occasion-driven: Mother's Day, Father's Day, Christmas, a birthday. Time-sensitive. Wants to give something that communicates care and effort rather than a generic gift.

Search behaviour: "meaningful memorial gift," "personalised portrait gift," "unique gift for grieving person," "thoughtful sympathy gift." More generic, category-level searches. Higher volume. Needs to be convinced that a portrait is the right gift category before evaluating Memorialize Art specifically.

Primary concern: Timing and gift-suitability. Will it arrive before the occasion? Will the recipient like it? Will it feel personal enough? What happens if something goes wrong?

Conversion catalyst: Deadline guarantees, before-and-after creative, recipient reaction videos, explicit delivery timelines tied to occasion dates.

The current account runs uniform creative to both buyer types. Separate asset groups with messaging specific to each ICP would improve ad relevance scores, improve Quality Score on targeted keyword groups, and lift conversion rates by serving the right message to the right intent signal. This is achievable within PMax through audience signal configuration and asset group segmentation.

Strategic Roadmap

Four Phases to Rebuild the Account

The roadmap is sequenced by dependency. Phases 1 and 2 address infrastructure that must be stable before increasing spend. Phase 3 scales on a clean foundation. Phase 4 compounds the gains across channels and markets.

Phase 1 - Weeks 1 to 2: Infrastructure Repair

Nothing gets turned on until these are done

Audit and rebuild conversion tracking: remove duplicate Google Shopping App events, switch all seven eligible events to Data-Driven Attribution, verify Add to Cart and Begin Checkout fire correctly via Google Tag Assistant, and standardise all campaigns to a single unified purchase conversion action. Resolve the Merchant Center invalid price issue and reactivate the feed to clear the "No campaigns" eligibility block. Conduct a full feed data quality audit. Reset all tROAS targets to realistic levels based on current performance data: the December 31 increase to 800% needs to be unwound before campaigns can spend effectively on relaunch. Target on delivery: verified tracking, eligible feed, functional relaunch parameters.

Phase 2 - Weeks 3 to 6: Account Rebuild

Consolidate, rebuild assets, relaunch at test budget

Consolidate five PMax campaigns into two: one feed-supplemented campaign for product-led acquisition and one asset-only campaign for brand-adjacent prospecting and retargeting. Rebuild all asset groups to Excellent strength with dedicated headlines, descriptions, and images for each ICP segment. Rebuild Brand Search with exact match brand terms only and an appropriate tROAS target of 800% minimum. Build dedicated retargeting campaigns for product page viewers and cart abandoners. Launch at $300 to $500 per day to accumulate clean signal data. Target: stable ROAS at 3.5x or higher before Phase 3 spend increase. Add bid schedule adjustments for 4 PM to 10 PM and Sunday-weighted multipliers.

Phase 3 - Weeks 7 to 12: Controlled Scale

Increase budgets on validated campaigns only

With four to six weeks of clean conversion data, begin scaling PMax and Search budgets at 15% to 20% per week on campaigns holding 4.0x ROAS or higher. Launch the Competitor Search conquest strategy with a dedicated landing page built specifically for visitors arriving from PaintYourLife brand queries: direct comparison, social proof, and a specific offer. Begin mobile speed optimisation with a target of reducing TBT below 200ms and Speed Index below 5 seconds, which should improve mobile conversion rate by an estimated 15% to 25%. Target: $700 to $1,000 per day at 4.0x to 4.5x ROAS.

Phase 4 - Months 4 to 12: Compound

Expand market, diversify channels, build the calendar

Expand into UK and Canada, where campaign naming history (Standard Search Mother's Day UK, Standard Search CA) indicates prior testing. Build a seasonal campaign calendar around Mother's Day, Father's Day, pet loss awareness events, Thanksgiving, and Christmas, each with dedicated creative and tailored offers. Explore Demand Gen campaigns for video-led prospecting using the emotional product story. Target: $2,000 or more per day at 4.5x ROAS across US, UK, and CA. Q4 2026 should represent the strongest quarter in the account's history with a full planning cycle in advance.
